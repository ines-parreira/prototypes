Copilot Design Principles
Seven rules that govern every Copilot design decision. Principles marked with a diamond are competitive differentiators versus Siena AI and similar copilots.
1
Arrive with a diagnosis, not a blank state ◆ Differentiator
vs. Siena: Siena offers generic entry points like "How can I help?" that don't change based on what's actually happening with the merchant's automation. Gorgias Copilot arrives already informed by the data — with a specific diagnosis, not a menu of prompts.
Copilot arrives informed by the data. Not "here are four things you could ask me" but "this automation had 34 handovers last week, mostly because the subscription check step is missing. Here's a revised version."
2
Show the work ◆ Differentiator
vs. Siena: Siena shows final answers with no reasoning trail — no indication of what data was checked, how conclusions were reached, or how confident the copilot is. Gorgias Copilot makes every step inspectable with collapsible reasoning and tiered action cards.
Every recommendation shows what data was used, what steps were taken, and why. Reasoning traces, data sources, confidence, and change history are always one click away. Understanding is built through interaction.
3
Lead with the answer, disclose progressively
Surface the most impactful insight first. Depth is available on pull, never dumped upfront. The merchant gets the action and the "so what" immediately — supporting evidence is a click away.
4
Center the artifact, not the chat ◆ Differentiator
vs. Siena: Siena separates diagnosis and action — analysis appears in chat, while editable guidance sits in a different panel with no diff, no preview, and no clear link between the flagged issue and the resulting draft. Gorgias Copilot centers the editable artifact with carried context and inline diff.
The editable artifact is the primary interaction surface. Drafts show inline diffs, carry the context that triggered them, and are approved on the artifact itself — not in a separate chat bubble.
5
Previewable, approvable, reversible ◆ Differentiator
vs. Siena: Siena has no visible approval workflow for copilot actions — no preview of what will change, no explicit confirmation step, no undo. Gorgias Copilot makes every state-changing action pass through a preview → approve → undo flow that builds merchant trust.
Every AI action shows what will happen before it happens, asks for confirmation, and offers undo. Read-actions and write-actions are visually distinct. The approval flow builds trust, not friction.
6
Close the loop ◆ Differentiator
vs. Siena: Both Siena and Gaia currently end with a ceremonial status badge ("Automation Updated", "Approved") — no estimated impact, no commitment to track outcomes, no learning signal. Gorgias Copilot should state expected impact upfront and follow through with monitoring.
After a change is published, Copilot states the expected impact and commits to monitoring. The system shows signals of learning — what worked, what changed, what improved since last time.
7
Global presence, local intelligence
Copilot is accessible from every product surface but deeply contextual to where you are and what you're doing. On AI Agent it's proactive. On Analytics it's diagnostic. Same system, different mode.
Immersive
Full-screen experience where the copilot takes over the main content area. Best for complex, multi-step workflows like guidance creation, skill building, or deep analysis tasks.

Assistive
Side panel that runs alongside the main workspace. The copilot provides suggestions, answers, and actions without taking over the screen. Best for real-time support during ticket handling.

Embedded
Inline components embedded directly within existing UI surfaces. Copilot outputs appear in context — inside cards, tables, or settings pages — without a separate panel or overlay.
